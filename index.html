Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

(1630–1680)

Shivaji Shahaji Bhonsale

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) was one of India's greatest rulers and the founder of the Maratha Empire. He is celebrated for his military genius, administrative acumen, and efforts to promote cultural and social reforms. Shivaji's legacy continues to inspire millions as a symbol of courage, self-governance, and justice.

  • Birth:Shivaji was born on February 19, 1630, in the hill-fort of Shivneri (near Pune) to Shahaji Bhosale and Jijabai.
  • Upbringing: His mother Jijabai played a pivotal role in shaping his character, teaching him the values of duty, justice, and devotion to Hindu culture.
  • Mentorship:Shivaji was trained in martial arts, governance, and warfare by skilled teachers like Dadoji Konddev.



Military Achievements


  1. Founder of the Maratha Empire:

    Shivaji established the Maratha kingdom at a time when the Mughal Empire and other powers dominated India. His empire spanned across present-day Maharashtra and parts of Karnataka, Gujarat, and Tamil Nadu.

  2. Guerrilla Warfare:

    Known as the "Father of Indian Guerrilla Warfare," Shivaji employed innovative tactics, such as hit-and-run attacks, ambushes, and the strategic use of forts.

  3. Conquest of Forts:

    Shivaji captured and built numerous forts, including Torna, Raigad, and Pratapgad, using them as strategic military bases. He built a naval fleet, making him one of the few Indian rulers to emphasize maritime defense.

  4. Battle with the Mughals:

    Shivaji resisted the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb, defeating some of his most formidable generals. His daring escape from Agra in 1666, where Aurangzeb had held him captive, is legendary.
